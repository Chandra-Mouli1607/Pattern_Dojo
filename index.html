What is CRT?

Every higher-timeframe candle is just a range of price action on a lower timeframe. Candle Range Theory says that range follows a predictable 3-act script — and if you can read it, you can trade the reversal before the crowd realizes the breakout was fake.

The 3-Act Play

  1. 1
    Accumulation: Price chops sideways inside the previous candle's high and low. Orders are being collected quietly.
  2. 2
    Manipulation: Price sweeps the liquidity above or below the range — a false breakout that traps retail traders and triggers their stops.
  3. 3
    Distribution: The real move begins. Price reverses and drives toward the opposite end of the range (or beyond).
CRT 3-Phase Model — Bullish Reversal

The golden rule: when one candle sweeps the previous candle's high or low and price returns inside the range, the real move is toward the opposite extreme.

What is Turtle Body Soup?

TBS is the entry method inside a CRT setup. The name is a play on the original Turtle Traders who traded breakouts — Turtle Soup flips that by fading false breakouts and "making soup out of the turtles."

The "body" part is critical: in TBS, the manipulation candle's body closes beyond the previous high or low. This traps more traders than a wick-only sweep, creating a higher-probability reversal.

TBS (Turtle Body Soup)

Body CLOSES beyond the level. Stronger trap = higher probability. More liquidity grabbed.

TWS (Turtle Wick Soup)

Only the WICK pokes past. Body stays inside. Weaker signal — fewer traders trapped.

How to Find the CRT Candle

You're looking for a 3-candle sequence on your HTF where the middle candle sweeps one side of the range and the third expands to the other.

  1. 1
    Pick your HTF candle — Daily, 4H, or 1H. Mark its high and low — that's your range.
  2. 2
    Watch Candle 2 — Does it break the high or low but close back inside the range? That's your manipulation = CRT trigger.
  3. 3
    Candle 3 = Distribution — Price expands toward the opposite extreme. This is where your trade lives.
CRT 3-Candle Identification — Bullish Setup
Pro Tip

The deeper the sweep wick and the stronger the close back inside the range, the higher probability. A candle that barely tags the level is weak. You want a convincing stab that grabs real liquidity — then a decisive rejection.

Good CRT Candle Checklist

  1. ✓
    Clear range: Candle 1 has a well-defined high and low with decent body size.
  2. ✓
    Sweep + rejection: Candle 2 pokes past one extreme but body closes back inside.
  3. ✓
    At a key level: The range sits at significant S/R (PDH, PDL, OB, FVG).
  4. ✓
    Session timing: Sweep happens during a killzone when volume supports the move.

How to Find Key Levels

A CRT without a key level is like fishing in a bathtub. Key levels are where stop orders cluster and institutions go hunting.

Liquidity Pools

PDH / PDL PWH / PWL PMH / PML Session H/L

Previous Day/Week/Month High/Low — where retail traders cluster their stops.

Order Blocks

The last bullish candle before a big drop (bearish OB) or the last bearish candle before a rally (bullish OB). These represent where institutional money entered.

Fair Value Gaps (FVGs)

Three-candle gaps where price moved so fast it left an imbalance. Price tends to return and fill these.

How to Find TBS

TBS is your precision entry. The key: the sweep candle's body closes beyond the previous level — not just the wick.

TBS Model #1 — Standard Entry

  1. 1
    Mark CRT Range: HTF candle's high and low = your boundaries.
  2. 2
    Wait for TBS Sweep: Next candle's BODY closes beyond the high or low.
  3. 3
    Confirm MSS on LTF: Drop to lower TF, look for a Market Structure Shift opposing the sweep direction.
  4. 4
    Enter at OB/FVG: After MSS, enter on the retest of the OB or FVG created by the reversal.
  5. 5
    SL & TP: SL beyond the sweep wick. TP at opposite CRT range extreme (100%).
TBS Model #1 — Bullish Entry (Bearish Body Sweep)
Min R:R — 1:2  |  Target — 1:3 to 1:5
TBS vs TWS

TBS (body close beyond) is the A+ setup. TWS (wick only) is valid but lower probability. Focus on TBS first. Context and key levels always matter more than the pattern alone.

Execution Workflow

1

HTF — Identify CRT Range

Find range candle at a key level. Mark high/low. Determine directional bias.

2

Mid-TF — Watch for TBS Sweep

Body closing beyond the range? TBS. Only wick? TWS (lower prob). Wait for the close.

3

LTF — Confirm MSS + Entry

Drop to 5m/1m. Find the Market Structure Shift. Enter at the OB or FVG retest.

4

Execute the Trade

Entry at OB/FVG. SL beyond sweep wick. TP1 at 50% EQ. TP2 at opposite extreme. Trail rest.
